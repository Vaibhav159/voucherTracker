Mastering the Art of Premium Travel Rewards

Written by Arjun Mehta • Updated Oct 24, 2023

location_on Tokyo, Japan - First Class Lounge

The allure of premium travel rewards isn't just about the occasional free flight; it's about systematically dismantling the cost of luxury. For the discerning Indian traveler, the landscape has shifted. The game is no longer about hoarding points—it's about velocity and valuation.

Most "gurus" will tell you to save for a rainy day. I'm here to tell you that points are a depreciating asset. Airlines devalue their charts annually. Hotel categories shift upwards. The only hedge against this inflation is an "earn and burn" strategy executed with precision.

"Your points balance is a liability on the bank's balance sheet. They want to reduce it. Your job is to extract maximum value before they do."

01. Transfer Partners vs. Portals

The cardinal sin of credit card rewards is redeeming points directly through a bank portal at a fixed rate (usually ₹0.50 to ₹1.00 per point). By transferring to partners like Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer or Virgin Atlantic, you can unlock values upwards of ₹4.00 per point.

lightbulb

Pro Tip: The Sweet Spot

Look for "Sweet Spots" in award charts. For example, using Virgin Atlantic miles to book ANA First Class from Tokyo to the US/Europe is often considered the best redemption in the world.

The path to luxury travel is paved with strategy, not just spending. Assess your goals, pick your ecosystem, and never let points sit idle. The world is waiting, and it's cheaper than you think.
